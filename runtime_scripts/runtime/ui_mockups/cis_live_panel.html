Blender cloth sim issue
Cloth sim collides correctly in viewport but bakes with artifacts on fast-moving frames. Happens above frame 120.
Round 1 — 09:14
You
Cloth sim collides fine in viewport but when I bake it, fast frames above 120 get mesh penetration artifacts. Quality is set to 5. Could this be a substep issue?
Claude
Yes — this is a substep problem. At Quality 5 Blender uses 5 substeps per frame, which is insufficient for fast-moving colliders. Increase substeps to 10–15 for the cloth object and match the collision quality on your collider mesh. Also check that your collision object has Cloth Collision enabled, not just regular collision.
Gemini
The artifacts above frame 120 suggest accumulated simulation error. Try baking from frame 1 each time — Blender cloth bakes are not resumable mid-cache. Also verify your collision object's scale is applied (Ctrl+A → Apply Scale) as unapplied scale causes collision calculation errors at speed.
ChatGPT
This is typically a combination of substeps and collision distance. Raise substeps to at least 10. Lower the collision distance on your cloth to 0.002–0.005 instead of the default 0.015. The default distance is too large for fast animation and causes early collision detection that looks like penetration on bake.
Round 2 — 09:31
You
Tried substeps 12, still happening. The collider is a character mesh that has rapid arm motion. Scale is applied.
Claude
For a character mesh collider, the armature deformation between frames may be too large for the cloth to track even at 12 substeps. Try increasing to 20 substeps. Also enable Self Collision on the cloth if not already active — rapid arm motion can cause the cloth to clip through itself first.
Gemini
The character deforming mesh is the core issue. Blender's cloth solver treats the collider as a static snapshot per substep — very fast arm movement means the mesh jumps between positions. Consider adding an inbetween keyframe on the armature around frame 118–122 to reduce per-frame delta.
ChatGPT
At this point substeps alone won't solve it. The real fix is to bake the character animation to shape keys before running cloth, or use a proxy collision mesh — a simplified low-poly version of the arm that moves identically but has smoother surface normals. The cloth solver handles low-poly colliders much better at speed.
Round 3 — in progress
You
The proxy collision mesh idea sounds right. How do I set that up — create a low poly arm shape, parent it to the armature?
Claude
Yes — create a simplified mesh (low poly capsule or rough arm shape), weight paint it to the same arm bones, and make it a collision object. Hide it from render. The cloth sim references the proxy, not the detailed mesh.
